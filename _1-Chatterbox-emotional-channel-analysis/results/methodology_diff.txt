METHODOLOGY DIFFERENCE: Zonos vs Chatterbox

1. EMOTION CONTROL
   Zonos: 8D emotion vector — each emotion (happy, sad, angry, etc.) has an independent
          weight. Can isolate and manipulate individual emotions.
   Chatterbox: Scalar exaggeration (0.0–1.0) — single intensity control. Cannot isolate
               individual emotions. All emotions are mapped to a single exaggeration value.

2. MAPPING
   Zonos: Direct emotion → vector weight mapping.
   Chatterbox: Emotion → exaggeration value (heuristic mapping):
     neutral=0.3, happy=0.7, sad=0.4, angry=0.9, fearful=0.8, surprised=0.85, low_energy=0.1
   This mapping is subjective and not grounded in Chatterbox documentation.

3. ACOUSTIC IMPLICATION
   Zonos: Can produce emotion-specific acoustic patterns (e.g., happy = higher pitch +
          faster tempo without changing timbre).
   Chatterbox: Exaggeration affects all dimensions simultaneously. Higher values increase
               expressiveness globally; lower values flatten everything.

4. COMPARABILITY
   The ablation classifier results are comparable (same algorithm, same validation).
   The heatmap z-scores are comparable in structure but may differ in magnitude due to
   the different control mechanisms.

This is a finding, not a flaw: scalar vs vector control is an architectural difference
that reveals how model design shapes the emotion-acoustic relationship.
