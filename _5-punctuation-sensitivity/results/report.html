Punctuation Sensitivity Probe

Descriptive acoustic analysis of punctuation-prosody effects across three TTS models
— Chatterbox, XTTS-v2, and Kokoro —
over 28 utterances spanning 5 punctuation categories.
This is a preliminary smoke test, not a validated benchmark.

1   Caveat

This report describes acoustic measurements from 28 hand-crafted utterances per model. It is not a benchmark. The sample size is too small for ranking claims. All metrics are acoustic proxies (VAD pauses, pyin F0) without perceptual validation. Confidence intervals and effect sizes are reported where possible; most comparisons have 2-6 data points per condition and should be treated as anecdotal.

2   Methodology

Stimuli. 28 utterances were manually designed to probe each punctuation category. Each utterance was rendered with the exact punctuation required (no textual variation beyond the target mark) to isolate the effect of punctuation on prosody.

Categories (5). Sentence-end (period, question mark, exclamation mark), pause hierarchy (comma, semicolon, em-dash, ellipsis), trailing punctuation (ellipsis vs period), quotation (quoted speech vs reported speech), and capitalisation (ALL CAPS, Title Case, lowercase).

Models (3). Chatterbox, XTTS-v2, and Kokoro were each used to generate 28 audio clips at 24 kHz. Chatterbox and XTTS-v2 use voice cloning from VCTK p229; Kokoro uses its default voice (no adaptation baseline).

Acoustic analysis. Voice activity detection uses energy-based VAD (threshold = 0.05 × max RMS, 20 ms window). F0 extracted with librosa.pyin (50-600 Hz, 256-hop). Pauses are RMS-below-threshold regions ≥30 ms. Internal pauses are those occurring before 95% of total duration. Terminal F0 slope computed over the final 400 ms via linear regression. 95% CIs are bootstrap percentile intervals (1000 resamples).

10   Methodological Limitations

  • Sample size. 28 utterances per model with 2-6 items per subcategory. Insufficient for statistical inference or model ranking.
  • No perceptual ground truth. All metrics are acoustic proxies (VAD pauses, pyin F0). No listening tests, MOS, ABX, or preference judgments were collected. These metrics have unknown correlation with human perception of prosody.
  • No forced alignment. Pauses are pooled across all internal RMS-detectable silences, not anchored to specific punctuation tokens. A silence anywhere in the utterance before 95% duration counts as an "internal pause" regardless of where punctuation occurs.
  • Crude VAD. Energy threshold (0.05 × max RMS) is fragile across models with different loudness, noise floor, vocoder artifacts, and trailing silence policies.
  • F0 extraction noise. librosa.pyin can fail on creaky voice, unvoiced endings, and vocoder artifacts in short generated utterances. README and code acknowledge this.
  • Arbitrary thresholds. Gate (150 ms), quotation shift (>10 Hz F0 range), and internal pause cutoff (95% duration) are all heuristic values without empirical justification.
  • No text normalization audit. TTS frontends may normalize punctuation before synthesis. This is not inspected or logged in the current pipeline.
  • Unfair model comparison. Kokoro lacks voice cloning and uses a different speaker, prosody prior, and conditioning mode. It serves as a "no-adaptation baseline" but should not be compared directly in rankings.
  • Capitalization test conflates factors. "STOP!" vs "Stop!" vs "stop!" differs in capitalization, token frequency, and potentially phoneme-level priors. n=1.
  • No punctuation-stripped controls. Without a baseline condition where punctuation is removed, we cannot isolate whether models respond to the punctuation mark itself or to tokenizer-side effects of the surrounding text.
